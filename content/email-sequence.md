## Email 2 — Day 2: The Watering Myth

**Subject:** Stop watering on Tuesdays
**Preview:** Your plants don't know what day it is

Hi {{first_name}},

Quick story. I killed a $40 fiddle-leaf fig by watering it on a schedule.

Every Tuesday. For six months. Like clockwork. The plant had no idea it was Tuesday.

What it actually needed was for the soil to dry out between waterings — and that depends on the light, the season, the pot size, the humidity. Not the day of the week.

So here's the rule I use now:

**Stick your finger 2cm into the soil. If it's dry, water. If it's damp, walk away.**

That's the whole secret. The cheat sheet you downloaded yesterday maps this out for every common houseplant — but the finger trick works for everything.

## Email 3 — Day 4: The Propagation Trick

**Subject:** The 90% mistake most propagators make
**Preview:** Hint: it's not about the cutting

Hi {{first_name}},

I propagated my first cutting in 2019. It took four months to root and then immediately rotted.

The mistake? I cut below the node — and the node is where roots grow from. I had a perfectly healthy stem sitting in water that was never going to grow anything.

Here's the correct way:

1. Identify a healthy stem with at least one **node** (the bump where leaves meet stem)
2. Cut **below** the node at a 45° angle
3. Remove any leaves that would sit below the water line
4. Place in clean water, change every 3 days
5. Wait. Patiently. Some plants root in a week, others take a month.

This is exactly the kind of thing our Propagation Diary is designed to track. Date taken, node position, medium, root date, first leaf date — so you can compare what works across cuttings.
